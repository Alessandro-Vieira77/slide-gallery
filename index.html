Mont Saint Michel
Mont Saint-Michel (French Mont-Saint-Michel) is a rocky island at the mouth of the Couesnon River in the Manche department of France, where an abbey (abbey of Mont Saint-Michel) and shrines were built in honour of the Archangel Saint Michael. Its former name is "Mont Saint-Michel in danger from the sea" (Mons Sancti Michaeli in periculo mari).
Uyuni Salt Flats
In Bolivia, it is the largest salt desert in the world, with an area of ​​approximately 12,000 km² and an altitude of 3,600 meters. It is known for its unique landscapes and the mirror effect that forms during the rainy season, when the salt flat is covered by a thin layer of water.
Atacama Sky
The Atacama Desert is an inhospitable region that will surprise you. Mummies, volcanoes and places that look like lunar and Martian terrains, it is a paradise for astronomers – and also for the curious. Have you always wanted to see amazing images of the sky through a telescope? The desert gives you that chance. There are 330 clear nights per year and one of the clearest skies on the entire planet.
Tianmen Mountain:
Heaven's Gate in China
Legend has it that over 1500 years ago, the Emperor of the Zhou Dynasty spent years searching for the perfect place to worship Heaven and found TianMen, which means the gate of heaven. From then on, the mountain became famous throughout the country.
Machu Picchu
Huayna Picchu Picchu or Machu Picchu (in Quechua Machu Picchu, "old mountain"), also called "lost city of the Incas", is a well-preserved Inca citadel from the pre-Columbian era, located on top of a mountain, 2,400 meters above sea level, in the Urubamba River valley, in present-day Peru.
Coliseum
The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an oval amphitheatre located in the centre of the city of Rome, the capital of Italy. Built of bricks covered with mortar and sand, and originally covered with travertine, it is the largest amphitheatre ever built and is situated to the east of the Roman Forum.
